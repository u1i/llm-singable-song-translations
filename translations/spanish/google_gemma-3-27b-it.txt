**Key Considerations & Explanations:**

*   **Syllable Count:** I've kept within the ±1 syllable rule for almost every line. Some slight adjustments were needed for natural phrasing.
*   **Rhyme Scheme:**  The original AABB rhyme scheme is maintained throughout.
*   **Natural Spanish:** I avoided overly literal translations that would sound awkward when sung. I used phrasing common in Spanish love songs.
*   **"Ser" vs. "Estar":**  I used both *ser* and *estar* appropriately to convey different aspects of being/feeling. *Estar* for temporary states (being with me), *ser* for inherent qualities (being my love).
*   **"Mirar" instead of "Ojos":**  Using "mirar" (gaze/look) felt more poetic and flowed better in the song context than directly translating "eyes."
*   **"Cariño" instead of "Amor":** While *amor* is perfectly valid, *cariño* can feel warmer and more intimate, fitting the tone. I've used both to avoid repetition.
*   **"Loco" for "Fool":**  "Fool" translates to *tonto*, but *loco* (crazy) is a more common and passionate expression of love in Spanish songs.
